Los Angeles businesses usually start with one question: **Do we need armed guards?** The answer depends on risk, environment, and the customer experience you want—not on what looks “tougher” in a brochure.

## When unarmed guards are often the right fit

Unarmed security is common for:

- Retail and mixed-use properties with frequent public interaction
- Corporate lobbies and visitor management
- Residential communities where professionalism and communication matter

Unarmed programs still deter crime through visibility, access control, and consistent reporting.

## When armed guards are more commonly considered

Armed coverage is more frequently evaluated for:

- High-value goods, large cash exposure, or elevated threat environments
- Sites with repeated serious incidents or clear elevated risk
- Certain overnight operations where deterrence posture is a priority

## The Los Angeles-specific angle

LA’s density and diversity mean “risk” is not uniform across neighborhoods or even blocks. A good provider will **walk the assumptions** with you: access points, blind spots, hours, and what your cameras already cover.

If you are unsure, start with a scoped assessment and ask for **two scenarios** in the proposal: armed and unarmed, with clear reasoning for each. Trivon Protection focuses on recommendations you can defend to leadership—not fear-based upsells.

Hiring private security in Los Angeles should feel like hiring an operations partner, not buying a commodity SKU. Use this checklist to compare providers consistently.

## 1) Scope clarity

Define hours, posts, access points, and escalation contacts before you review pricing. If vendors are guessing, their quotes will not be comparable.

## 2) Licensing and compliance posture

Confirm how the vendor handles **California BSIS** licensing requirements for guards and how credentials are verified before shifts start.

## 3) Supervision and replacements

Ask what happens on callouts, no-shows, and performance issues. The answer should include field leadership, not vague promises.

## 4) Reporting you can use

Daily activity reporting should be usable by property management and security leadership, not a pile of meaningless checkboxes.

## 5) Contract transparency

You should see how overtime, holidays, and schedule changes are handled. Surprises here are where “cheap” programs become expensive.

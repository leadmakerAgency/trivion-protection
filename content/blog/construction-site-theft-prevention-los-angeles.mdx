Los Angeles construction sites attract theft because they concentrate **high-value materials**, **open access**, and **predictable quiet windows**. Cameras help investigations, but deterrence and access discipline are what prevent losses in the first place.

## Start with access reality

If gates fail, fences gap, or deliveries are unchecked, you will lose copper, tools, and equipment regardless of how many cameras you install. Security should own the access workflow with clear verification steps.

## Patrol design matters more than “more guards”

Randomized routes, blind-spot touchpoints, and documented rounds reduce predictable gaps. Good programs also integrate with site leadership so changes in phase (vertical vs finish) update post orders.

## Documentation is ROI

When theft happens, the fastest recoveries usually involve strong documentation: timestamps, entry logs, and clear incident narratives. That is not extra paperwork—it is how you protect schedule and budget.
